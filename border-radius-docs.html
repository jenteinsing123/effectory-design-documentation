Border Radius

Corner rounding sets the tone of a surface — from sharp data grids to soft, friendly cards. Engage uses six named steps plus a full pill radius. Larger containers take larger radii; small controls stay crisp.

🔲 Scale

radius-none Data tables · dense lists · full-bleed charts
0 px
radius-sm Inputs · small buttons · icon-buttons · tags
4 px
radius-base Default buttons · segmented controls · badges
6 px
radius-md Navigation items · menu rows · small cards
8 px
radius-lg Cards · panels · popovers
12 px
radius-xl Modals · dialogs · large feature cards
16 px
radius-full Pills · avatars · round icon buttons · toggles
full
Rule of thumb — pick the radius by container size: tight controls get radius-sm, grouped surfaces get radius-lg, overlays get radius-xl, and anything circular uses radius-full.

✨ Guidance

Scale the radius with the container

A 12 px radius on a 32 px button looks bloated; a 4 px radius on a 600 px modal looks severe. As elements grow, step up the radius so the curvature stays visually consistent.

Nest tighter inside softer

Children should have a smaller radius than their parent. A radius-base button inside a radius-lg card reads cleanly; the reverse creates an awkward gap between the corner shapes.

Stay on the named steps

Don't introduce one-off values like 5, 7, or 10 px. The scale is deliberately short — if nothing fits, it usually means the element is in the wrong size bucket, not the wrong radius.
